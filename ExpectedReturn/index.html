v0.9.6 — Median as Default

The default earnings-growth mode is now the per-country median across the three approaches (GDP × β, LT EPS, SGR). Median was chosen over mean because it's robust to single-mode outliers: when one of the three approaches is clearly wrong for a given country (SGR distorted by a depressed book-value base, GDP × β missing buybacks, LT EPS contaminated by a one-off cycle), the median throws out that outlier and keeps the two that agree. The user can always flip to a single mode to interrogate which one is driving any disagreement.

What Each Mode Means

GDP × β  (legacy)
  earnings growth = (gdp × λg + inflation) × βGDP

LT EPS  (realized)
  earnings growth = realized 10y EPS CAGR

SGR  (structural)
  earnings growth = ROE × (1 − payout)

Median  (default)
  earnings growth = median(GDP × β, LT EPS, SGR)

Why Multiplicative FX

The legacy framework added ΔFX to local return: r = (FCF + growth + ΔFX) × haircut. That's a linearization that's only accurate at small numbers. For Turkey with ~20% local return and ~−24% annual FX, the additive version gives roughly −4%, while the correct multiplicative answer is 1.20 × 0.76 − 1 = −9%. The gap matters most exactly where it's hardest to get right — high-inflation EM.

Bank Haircut on Local Return

The haircut (1 − 0.4 × wfin) applies to local return before FX translation. Bank concentration is a local-equity-quality issue; FX is a separate mechanical translation. Applying the haircut after FX would have meant currency depreciation reduces the apparent bank-concentration penalty, which makes no economic sense.

What's Still Judgmental

The λ ladder values (only affects GDP × β mode), the −3% CAD threshold, the 0.4 coefficient in the bank haircut, the choice of 10-year windows for ROE and LT EPS, and the underlying assumption that the next decade resembles the last. Every one of these is exposed as either a UI toggle or an editable cell — click any input to override.

FCF Yield · Realized Anchor

The honest input. c / PE. Already-earned cash. Forecast error ≈ zero, modulo the c-stability question for distressed-recovery markets.

Earnings Growth · Median Default

Mode toggle drives this column. Median collapses three views into one robust number. Switch to a single mode to see where they disagree.

FX · Observable Economics

10y annualized FX move from the spreadsheet as the structural ΔFX; CAD overshoot adds drag if deficit < −3%. No confidence multiplier — it's mechanical translation, not a forecast.

Haircut · Local-Currency Discount

(1 − 0.4 × wfin) applied to local return before FX. Penalizes bank concentration in local equity quality, not in the currency translation.
