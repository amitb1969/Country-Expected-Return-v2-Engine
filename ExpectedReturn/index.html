v0.9.7 spec

What Changed vs v0.9.4

Three structural revisions and one functional addition.

1 · Earnings Growth — Four Modes

v0.9.4 built earnings growth from (g_real × λ_g + π) × β_EPS. That construction has two problems: most listed-equity indices aren't a function of domestic GDP (SPX is 40%+ foreign revenue, KOSPI is dominated by Samsung/Hynix, TASI is Aramco), and the β_EPS coefficients were admittedly mock estimates. v0.9.7 exposes three alternative growth inputs and uses the per-country median as the default.

GDP × β : earnings growth = (gdp × λ_g + inflation) × β_GDP LT EPS : earnings growth = realized 10y EPS CAGR SGR : earnings growth = ROE × (1 − payout) Median : earnings growth = median(GDP×β, LT EPS, SGR) ← default

Median is robust to single-mode outliers — when one approach is clearly wrong for a given country (SGR distorted by depressed book value, GDP × β missing buybacks, LT EPS contaminated by a cycle base year), the median throws out the outlier and keeps the two that agree.

2 · Multiplicative FX Translation

v0.9.4 added ΔFX to the local return. That's a linearization accurate at small numbers, wrong at high inflation. For Turkey with ~20% local return and ~−24% annual FX, additive gives roughly −4%; the correct multiplicative answer is 1.20 × 0.76 − 1 = −9%. v0.9.7 uses the correct compounding form.

local return = FCF yield + earnings growth local adj = local return × (1 − 0.4 × bank weight) r_USD = (1 + local adj) × (1 + ΔFX) − 1

3 · Bank Haircut on Local Return

v0.9.4 applied the haircut after FX. v0.9.7 applies it to local return before FX. Bank concentration is a local-equity-quality discount; FX is a separate mechanical translation. Applying the haircut after FX means currency depreciation reduces the apparent bank penalty — economically nonsensical.

4 · Live Commodity Context

The commodity panel now fetches YoY % from a Vercel serverless function (/api/commodities) which pulls Yahoo Finance via yfinance. Brent (BZ=F), Copper (HG=F), Iron Ore (SGX proxy via SCO.AX), Bloomberg Ag (^BCOMAG), Bloomberg Commodity broad (^BCOM). Cached 1 hour at the CDN. Numerical commodity impacts remain zeroed in the math — the panel drives only the qualitative Forecast Risk arrow per country.

What's Kept From v0.9.4

Tier system (US / DM / EM / EMHR), λ predictability ladder (only affects GDP × β mode and therefore the median that includes it), CAD overshoot logic (FX drag fires below −3% of GDP), FCF yield as the realized cash anchor, hurdle = r_SPY × (λ_SPY / λ_country).

What's Still Judgmental

λ values (Conservative / Standard / Aggressive toggle), the −3% CAD threshold and k=1.0 coefficient, the 0.4 coefficient in the bank haircut, the choice of 10y windows for ROE and LT EPS, and the assumption that the next decade resembles the last. Every one is exposed as a UI toggle or an editable cell — click anything to override.

The c > 1 Caveat (Greece, etc.)

A conversion ratio above 1 means FCF exceeds Net Income — typically because depreciation runs above capex (the business is underinvesting and liquidating its asset base) or because non-cash charges depress reported earnings. The framework treats c as a structural input, but in distressed-recovery markets the fat FCF yield it produces can be a melting-ice-cube signal rather than a defensive anchor. Apply judgment.

The SGR Caveat

SGR = ROE × (1 − payout) assumes current ROE is sustainable. For markets emerging from distress, ROE is sometimes inflated by a depressed book-value base from prior write-downs rather than by genuine earning power. The framework displays raw SGR; the user is expected to recognize when this matters (Greece, Turkey post-2018, parts of Brazil).
